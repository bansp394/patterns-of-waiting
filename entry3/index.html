The Wait Between Sips

You stand in a line that coils toward the counter. The grinder rumbles, milk hisses, cups click against the bar. Coats brush past; a door opens and closes with a bell that feels older than the room. You’re waiting for something small but steady the warm anchor that starts your morning and slows it down at the same time.

You catch your reflection in the pastry case, blurred by the fog of breath and heat. You check your phone; a minute passes, then two. It feels longer. The person ahead of you changes their order; the barista smiles anyway, writing another name with calm, practiced handwriting. In this little pause, the city moves around you without asking you to move with it. For a moment, you’re allowed to stand still.

What are you waiting for today?
Do you notice the world while you wait, or does it blur in thoughts?
Does this pause belong to you, or to the city?
