WAITING WAITING WAITING

STILL STILL STILL

You sit on the grass at Central Park, surrounded by a sea of people. Blankets spread, snacks shared, cameras ready. The sky is slowly changing colors blue fading into pink, then into the deep violet of early evening. Around you, laughter, chatter, and the low hum of speakers fill the air. Everyone is waiting for the same thing: the music to begin.

You check your phone. It’s been forty minutes since the gates opened, but somehow time feels both fast and painfully slow. The anticipation has a pulse a rhythm that everyone seems to move to without realizing. A stranger beside you hums a tune; someone else waves a flag; others scroll endlessly, refreshing feeds for updates.

What are you waiting for?
How long have you been waiting?
How did the waiting feel?
